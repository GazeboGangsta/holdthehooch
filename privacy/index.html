Privacy Policy — Hold the Hooch

Effective date: 21 April 2026  ·  Last updated: 21 April 2026

This privacy policy applies to the Hold the Hooch mobile app ("the app") for iOS and Android, and to the companion web game at https://gurgles.beer ("the website"). Both are operated by Callum Siciliano ("we", "us") as a personal/hobby project.

We have tried to build Hold the Hooch so that it collects as little information as possible. This policy describes what is collected, why, and what we do with it.

1. What we collect

The app collects and transmits only the following information when you submit a score to the global leaderboard:

  • Display name — a short name you choose yourself (1–20 characters) to appear on the leaderboard. This is never pre-filled, never linked to any email address or account, and does not need to be your real name.
  • Score — the final numeric score of a run.
  • Platform and app version — e.g. ios / android and the installed version string (used for debugging and release tracking).

The app does not collect:

  • Your email address, phone number, or any contact details.
  • Your real name (unless you choose to enter it as your display name).
  • Your location, IP-based location, or GPS data.
  • Your contacts, photos, microphone, camera, or files.
  • Any advertising identifier (IDFA / AAID).
  • Any analytics, crash reporting, or behavioural tracking data.
  • Any payment or financial information.

The app contains no advertising and no in-app purchases.

2. What is stored on your device

Your chosen display name is saved locally on your device so that you do not need to type it again on future runs. If a score submission fails (e.g. no network), it is saved locally and retried on the next app launch. These are stored in the app's private storage and never leave your device except as described in section 1.

Uninstalling the app removes this local data.

3. How your data is used

  • The display name and score are shown on the public global leaderboard in the app and at https://gurgles.beer. Anyone can see them.
  • Platform and version are used privately to diagnose bugs and track which app builds submitted which scores.
  • We do not sell, rent, or share your data with any third party for marketing purposes.

4. Where your data is stored

Scores and display names are stored in a SQLite database on the server hosting https://gurgles.beer. Requests are sent over HTTPS.

5. Third parties

We do not use any third-party analytics, advertising, social-login, or crash-reporting SDKs in the app.

The app is distributed through the Apple App Store and Google Play Store. Those platforms collect their own data (such as download counts and crash reports for developers) governed by their respective privacy policies:

  • Apple: https://www.apple.com/legal/privacy/
  • Google: https://policies.google.com/privacy

6. Children's privacy

Hold the Hooch is suitable for general audiences but is not specifically directed at children under 13. Because the leaderboard is public, we ask that players do not enter real names, email addresses, phone numbers, or any other personally identifying information as their display name.

We do not knowingly collect any information from children that would require parental consent under COPPA, GDPR-K, or similar laws. If you believe a child has submitted identifying information, contact us at the email below and we will remove the entry.

7. Your rights

You can ask us to remove your display name and score from the leaderboard at any time. Send an email to hello@thenatzeroes.com describing the entry (approximate date, display name, score) and we will delete it.

Because we do not collect any personally identifying information, we cannot verify that a deletion request comes from the original player. Requests will be processed in good faith.

If you are in the UK/EU, you also have rights under the UK GDPR / EU GDPR including the right to access, correct, or erase personal data, and to lodge a complaint with your local data protection authority. Because we collect no identifying data, the display name on the leaderboard is the only item we hold that could relate to an individual.

8. Data retention

Leaderboard entries (display name + score + platform + version + server timestamp) are retained indefinitely so the historical leaderboard stays intact. You can request deletion of your own entries as described in section 7.

9. Changes to this policy

If we change how the app handles data, we will update this page and change the "Last updated" date at the top. Significant changes will be noted in the app's store listing on the next release.
